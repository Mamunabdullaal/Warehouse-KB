Initial Warehouse & General Processing Rules

1. Initial Warehouse Quality Check - Fewer Items Returned

Purpose: To ensure items meet quality standards upon arrival at the warehouse, especially when fewer items are returned than expected.

Key Steps (Conceptual):

  • Receiving & Initial Scan: How returned items are logged into the system.
  • Item Count Verification: Comparing the physical count of returned items with the expected quantity.
  • Discrepancy Handling: Steps to take if the count doesn't match (e.g., investigation, reporting).
  • Quality Assessment: Basic checks for item condition (damaged, used, etc.).
  • Documentation: Recording all findings and actions.

4.1. General Processing Procedure for Returns & Appeals

This procedure helps customer service review returns and consumer appeals, especially for initial warehouse quality checks.

Key Steps:

  • Step 1: Is the Warehouse Proof Good Enough?
    • Choose "Yes" if:
      • The video clearly shows the item being unpacked.
      • OR, the warehouse sent photos (no video), and the photos clearly show the returned item(s).
    • Choose "No" if:
      • The warehouse sent no video or photos.
      • OR, photos were sent (no video), but they don't clearly show the item(s).
      • OR, video/photos were sent, but you can't tell if the item(s) belong to this return.
      • OR, the camera angle is wrong, or the person is doing things out of view.
    • Important: If you choose "No," do not go to Step 2.
  • Step 2: Check How Many Items the Warehouse Received.
    • Action: Look at the video and write down how many items the warehouse got.
    • Items clearly shown or suspected to be returned in the video are both counted as returned.
    • If no unpacking is shown, but the box is big enough for the item(s), count them as returned.
  • Step 3: Confirm Item Count.
    • Action: Make sure you have finished counting and checking the items.
  • Step 4: Add a Note and Finish.
    • Suggested Notes:
      • "Invalid video."
      • "Valid video, Item(s) verified."
      • "Valid video, Item(s) unable to be verified."
      • "Valid video, item unable to be found."

Wrong Package Handling Rules

2. Wrong Package Second Review - External

Purpose: To conduct a secondary review of packages flagged as incorrect, focusing on external details before internal inspection.

Key Steps (Conceptual):

  • Package Identification: Confirming the package as a "wrong package" based on initial flags.
  • External Label Check: Verifying shipping labels, recipient information, and tracking details.
  • Integrity Check: Assessing external packaging for tampering or damage.
  • System Cross-Check: Comparing external details with order records.
  • Next Action: Deciding if further internal review is needed or if the package can be routed.

5. Processing Rules: Package & Shipping Label Verification (Wrong Package Focus)

Purpose: To accurately verify package and shipping labels for "wrong package" claims.

Important Notes:

  • Package labels and shipping labels are separate. Don't mix their numbers.
  • Check ALL customer photos for each step, as they might be mixed up.

Key Steps:

  • Step 1: Labels from the Same Package?
    • Action: If NOT from the same package (e.g., shipping label on bag, package label on box), select "Not uploaded."
    • Action: If from the same package or unsure, go to Step 2.
  • Step 2: Is Logistics Tracking Number Correct?
    • Action: Check shipping label photo against the tracking number.
    • Mismatched/Fake: If they don't match, or photo is clearly fake/edited, select "Not uploaded" for both labels.
    • Match: Select "Yes."
    • Unclear: If shipping label photo is unclear, select "Not uploaded" for shipping label.
  • Step 3: Is Package Number Correct?
    • Action: Check package label photo against the package number and "packing time" (time on label should match packing time, usually same day).
    • Consistent (Label & Time): Select "Yes."
    • Label OK, Time Different: Select "Not uploaded."
    • Label & Time Inconsistent (No Editing): Select "No."
    • Unclear: If package label photo is unclear, select "Not uploaded."
    • Incomplete Label, Visible Part Doesn't Match, Time Consistent: Select "No."
    • Incomplete Label, Cannot Confirm Match: Select "Not uploaded."
    • Fake Package Label: Select "Not uploaded" for both, note "Evidence appears to be fraudulent."
    • Note: If shipping label covers package label's time, you can ignore checking packing time.

Local Merchant & General Return Rules

3. Local Merchants: Second Review of Merchant Appeal for Allegedly Missing Items in Returned Goods

Purpose: To re-evaluate appeals from local merchants claiming items are missing from returned goods. This ensures fair resolution in disputes.

Key Steps for Reviewing Appeals:

  • Step 1: Get the Appeal Details:
    • Record the merchant's appeal.
    • Collect all proof they sent (like photos or videos).
  • Step 2: Check Initial Information:
    • Compare what the merchant says with our return records.
  • Step 3: Look at the Proof Carefully:
    • Examine all photos, videos, or documents the merchant provided.
  • Step 4: Verify Internally (if needed):
    • Check our warehouse records.
    • Do a physical check if necessary.
  • Step 5: Make a Decision and Tell the Merchant:
    • Decide on the appeal.
    • Inform the merchant about the final decision.

6. Merchant Rejection Reasons (Local Merchants)

This section explains why merchants can refuse a return.

Valid Reasons for Rejection:

  • Reason 1: Returned Item Does Not Match
    • What it means: The customer sent back an item that is different from what was originally sold or sent to them.
    • Your Action (Customer Service):
      • Check the order details, what the customer asked for, and any proof (evidence).
      • If the customer received a wrong, damaged, or faulty item from us, then you should support the customer.
  • Reason 2: Returned Item Damaged
    • What it means: The customer sent back an item that is damaged, looks used, or cannot be sold again.

7. General Processing Steps & Proof Standards (Local Merchants)

This guide helps customer service process different return situations and explains what kind of proof is needed.

How to Use the Annotation Center:

  • Step 1: Mark the Return Status (for the whole order after it's returned):
    • If "No Item Returned": Choose `[All items not received (or returned items are not purchased items)]`. No more details are needed for this step.
    • If "Fully/Partially Returned": Choose `[Receive all/part of your purchase]`. This will open up more details for Step 2.
    • How to Decide: Use the customer's appeals, order information, proof from the merchant or warehouse, and any information the merchant has already reviewed.
      • If there's no `[merchant reviewed]` info: Make your decision based on the customer's appeal and the merchant's proof.
      • If there is `[merchant reviewed]` info: Use that information to make a full decision.
    • Important: Always read any notes from the merchant. If chat records show that both sides agreed on a solution, follow that agreement.
  • Step 2: Mark Items that Affect or Don't Affect Future Sales:
    • Action: If items were fully or partly returned, sort them into categories based on the `Proof Standards` below.
    • Remember: The total number of items that `Affect Resale` plus `Don't Affect Resale` must be LESS THAN the total number of items the customer asked for a refund on.

Proof Standards & Reasons for Rejection:

  • General Rejection Reasons: These apply when the merchant received the package but claims it was empty, damaged, or contained wrong items.
  • Special Case: Shipping Label Changes:
    • Scenario: A customer changes a tracking number, and the shipping company (like UPS) adds a correction label.
    • Action: Verify original label, correction label, and system info. If original label is incorrect, user is responsible.
  • Reshipment (Updated Oct 18, 2024):
    • If merchant says the whole item was sent again (reshipped) and has proof of delivery: Support the merchant.
    • Online Reshipment: If the customer started the reshipment, and our system (workbench) shows it was delivered and signed for, support the merchant.
    • Offline Reshipment: If the merchant and customer agreed to reship outside our system (no workbench info), the merchant must provide:
      • Proof of shipment (tracking shows it was delivered and signed for).
      • Proof of their agreement (e.g., screenshots of chat messages).
      • Both are needed to support the merchant.
  • Special Case: Package Size/Weight Doesn't Match:
    • Scenario: If proof (like a merchant's unboxing video) clearly shows the package size or weight doesn't match what it should be.
    • Action: Use your common sense to decide.
  • Duplicate Image Detected:
    • Scenario: If the warehouse proof has a `[Duplicate Image Detected]` label.
    • Action: Mark the return as `[Received All/Partial Goods]`.
    • Exception: If the duplicate is just a sample product picture, it doesn't count as the same.
  • Items Not Returned:
    • Scenario: Customer mailed package, tracking shows delivered, but merchant's warehouse claims not received.
    • Mark as "All items not received..." ONLY IF proven:
      • Incorrect return address (package received at non-warehouse address). (Updated July 17, 2024: If logistics address doesn't match historical or latest warehouse address.)
      • (Updated Sep 19, 2024): Temu label used, but tracking shows delivery address changed by user's actions.
    • Proof that is NOT valid for "Received all/part...":
      • Information from other platforms (e.g., Amazon warehouse didn't get it).
      • Screenshots of a warehouse search page that can't find the package.
    • Merchant says not received (distance rule - Updated Sep 13, 2024): If the delivery address from the shipping company is less than 5 miles from the merchant's warehouse, the merchant's proof is not valid.
    • Tracking only to City/Country (Updated Mar 10, 2025): For C3-C5 customers (or C1-C2 with risk control pass) not flagged for risk and no user-side changes in tracking, support the customer if the delivery address is just the city/country of the warehouse.
    • Action: After marking as `[received all/part...]`, go to Step 2 to check for damage (usually `[number of items not affecting resale]` if there are no other documents).
  • Return Empty Package:
    • Scenario: Merchant warehouse got a package, but it was empty.
    • Mark as "All items not received..." ONLY IF proven:
      • The signed package was truly sent by the customer (return slip, photos). (For our own warehouses, we are less strict about the shipping label in the unboxing video/photo).
      • The package was empty (unboxing photos).
    • If NOT proven: Mark as "Received all/part of the purchased items."
    • Merchant Weight Screenshots (Updated July 9): If the merchant's screenshot of the shipping company's website weight exactly matches the expected weight on our system, their proof is not valid. Mark as "Received all/part of the purchased goods."
    • Action: After marking as `[received all/part...]`, go to Step 2 to check for damage.
  • Returned Item Does Not Match:
    • Scenario: Merchant warehouse got a package, but the item inside doesn't match the type or model ordered.
    • Mark as "All items not received..." ONLY IF proven:
      • The signed package was truly sent by the customer (return slip, photos). (For our own warehouses, we are less strict about the shipping label in the unboxing video/photo).
      • The item or item model doesn't match the order (photos of the returned item).
      • The returned item's look doesn't match the product details.
    • If NOT proven: Mark as "Received all/part of the purchased items."
    • Action: After marking as `[received all/part...]`, go to Step 2 to check for damage.
    • Customer says they got the wrong item and want after-sales service:
      • Step 1.1: Check if returned items match customer's wrong item description. If NOT consistent, mark `[All items have not been received...]`.
      • Step 1.2: If consistent, check if warehouse has proof of NOT sending wrong item (e.g., packing video). If YES, mark `[All items have not been received...]`.
      • Step 1.2 (Alternate): If warehouse has NO proof of not sending wrong item, or proof is invalid, mark `[Received all/part of the purchased item]`.
    • Action: After marking as `[received all/part...]`, go to Step 2 to check for damage.
  • Correct Item Was Sent (Only for "Wrong Item" claims):
    • If the customer requests for wrong item after-sales, the annotation item may not match the item evidenced by the warehouse. Check if the warehouse has evidence that the correct items were sent (any one of the following is acceptable):
    • Merchant's shipping video: Must clearly show the items inside the package and the tracking number to confirm the package's identity.
    • Shipping photo and shipping label photo: The shipping photo must clearly show the items inside the package, and the tracking number on the label must match the tracking number in the order.
    • Shipping list: For example, a screenshot of a third-party platform's shipping details page, including the product name(s) and tracking details.
    • If the warehouse can provide any of the above evidence that the correct item was shipped, label the case as `[All items not received (or returned items are not the purchased items)]`.
    • If the warehouse has evidence that the correct items were sent (such as a video/screenshot of the packaging), and the evidence can prove that the items sent by the merchant are the same as those purchased by the user:
    • The package sent is indeed the package of the user's complaint order (return package label; return package photo;)
    • Note: If it is returned to a self-operated warehouse, this standard can be relaxed appropriately, and it is not mandatory for the quality inspection video/photo of the self-operated warehouse to show the package's logistics label.
    • Step 1.2: If the item returned by the customer is the same as the item described in the customer's after-sales request/appeal record, check whether the warehouse has any evidence that the correct item was sent.
    • Merchant's shipping video: Must clearly show the items inside the package and the tracking number to confirm the package's identity.
    • Shipping photo and shipping label photo: The shipping photo must clearly show the items inside the package, and the tracking number on the label must match the tracking number in the order.
    • Shipping list: For example, a screenshot of a third-party platform's shipping details page, including the product name(s) and tracking details.
    • If the warehouse cannot provide any of the above evidence, or the provided evidence is invalid, label the case as `[Received all/part of the purchased item]`.
    • After marking `[Received all/part of the purchased items]`, please further determine whether the items are damaged and affect resale according to Step 2.
  • Returned Item Damaged / Other Rejection Reasons (Resulting in "Received all/part..." in Step 1):
    • Special Case (Customer reported quality/defect): If merchant's damage reason is user's original after-sales reason (quality/defect), then product damage not user's fault. Judge based on remarks/order records.
    • Special Case (Customer reported missing item/accessory): If merchant's evidence shows missing major component, and user's reason was missing item/accessory. Judge comprehensively.
    • Scenario: Merchant received return, item damaged or missing major accessories, affecting secondary sales.
    • Label as "Number of items affecting resale" ONLY IF proven:
      • Signed package genuinely sent by user.
      • Item genuinely damaged (detailed photos/videos, e.g., rusted pot).
      • Damaged item is part of the order (overall photos).
    • If proof doesn't show damage: Label as "Number of items not affecting resale."
    • Note: External packaging damage, minor wheel wear are normal; label as "Number of items not affecting resale."
    • Special Case (User reported damage consistent with merchant): If merchant's damage record matches user's report (damage not user-caused), label as `[number of items not affecting resale]`.
  • Image Annotation Rules (Local Merchants - CS Follow-up Review):
    • New Label: "The merchant's evidence is valid and the pictures displayed to consumers are marked."
    • When supporting merchant rejection, new labels are added for `[None of the items were received...]` and `[Received all/part...]` (where items affecting resale > 0).
    • Annotation Options:
      • Valid, suitable for customer display: Compliant evidence images.
      • Valid, not suitable for consumer display: Compliant but restricted (faces, backend screenshots, Chinese screenshots, logistics screenshots, high rejection rate merchant images, similar image tags).
      • Invalid: Cannot support rejection (sample images, warehouse query pages).
    • High-frequency evidence types: Package shipping label, Logistics tracking website, Product photos, Outer packaging photos.
  • Rules for Marking Used Products:
    • First Rule: Merchant MUST have actual product photos in details. If not, reject merchant.
    • If merchant's feedback on secondary sales matches actual photos/condition description, it's not consumer's fault. Reject merchant.
    • Note: For "item not returned," "correct items sent," "empty package returned," rules are unchanged. Deviation only for `returned items are damaged` or `returned items do not match`. Only Step 2 `[Number of items affecting resale]` is different.

13. High Rejection Rate Merchants

Background: Some local or semi-managed merchants intentionally cause problems by unfairly refusing after-sales service. These merchants are given a `[high rejection rate merchant]` label.

What these merchants often do:

  • They don't provide a clear return label, so it's hard to prove the customer actually sent the package back.
  • They use the same proof (evidence) for many different cases.

Reasons they reject after-sales & how their proof usually looks:

  • Claim: Customer returned an empty package:
    • Proof: They only show one picture of an empty package, but it can't be proven that this specific package was sent back by the current customer.
  • Claim: Returned item does not match:
    • Proof: They only show one video of a return package being opened, but the return label info isn't clear.
    • Proof: They show 1 return label and 1 photo of the opened package, but these two pieces of proof don't clearly connect to each other.
    • Proof: They show a new-looking return label and a video of an opened package, but the label in the video isn't readable, and it can't be proven that this return was from the current customer.
  • Claim: Returned item is damaged:
    • Proof: They only show one picture of a damaged or clearly used product, but it can't be proven that this specific item was sent back by the current customer.

How to Process Tickets for High Rejection Rate Merchants:

  • Rule : For claims like "empty package," "mismatched item," or "correct item already sent":
    • To mark the case as `[None of the items has been received (or the returned items are not the purchased items)]`, the merchant must meet ALL of the following conditions, *in addition* to the normal evidence rules from "7. General Processing Steps & Proof Standards":
      • They provide a clear return label that matches the "return tracking number" shown on our system (workbench).
      • They can prove that the returned items don't match or that the package was empty, and this was confirmed by watching the unboxing of the package sent by the customer. (The unboxing video must be complete and continuous, showing the entire process.)
    • Otherwise (if not all conditions above are met): You must mark the case as `[All/some of the purchased items have been received]`.
  • Rule : For claims like "Returned item damaged" or other rejection reasons:
    • To mark the case as `[All / some of the purchased items have been received]` and fill in the "Number of items that affect resale" and "Number of items that do not affect resale" based on facts, the merchant must meet ALL of the following conditions, *in addition* to the normal evidence rules from "7. General Processing Steps & Proof Standards":
      • They provide a clear return label that matches the "return tracking number" shown on our system (workbench).
      • They can prove that the returned items are damaged or affect resale, and this was confirmed by watching the unboxing of the package sent by the customer. (The unboxing video must be complete and continuous, showing the entire process.)
    • Otherwise (if not all conditions above are met): You must mark "All / some of the purchased items have been received" and set "Number of items that affect resale" to 0.

12. High-Value Electronic Consumer Goods (with SN code) Review Process

Background: Addresses issues with users returning high-value items with mismatched SN codes. (How to determine if it is an SN code product? -> There is an "SN code product" label under the item name.)

Merchant Evidence Requirements:

  • Merchants must provide both types of evidence materials for shipment and return (both are indispensable).
  • The merchant's reason for rejection is: The returned product does not match! (Other rejection reasons will be handled as usual).
  • Both the proof of shipping and return need to be provided simultaneously.

Requirements for Supporting Documents:

  • Proof of Shipping: Accepted if any one of the following conditions is met:
    • The shipping outer packaging information: The shipping label and SN code must be clearly visible at the same time.
    • The return outer packaging information: The shipping label and SN code must be clearly seen at the same time. (The shipping label and SN code must be on the same page.)
  • Proof of Return: Must meet the following conditions simultaneously:
    • Returned outer packaging information: The return shipping label must be clearly visible.
    • A complete video of the machine being taken out of the packaging, and the machine's serial number can be clearly seen.

Review Requirements for Customer Service:

  • When the user returns the item in a non-original packaging box/returns the product in the original packaging box but the shipping label information and SN code cannot be verified:
    • If the following four points can be proven, annotate as follows: `[All items have not been received (or none of the returned items are the purchased items)]`, otherwise annotate as `[Received all/part of the purchased items]`:
      • The shipping package tracking number and the PO details match.
      • The shipping tracking number and the SN code are displayed on the same page.
      • The return package tracking number and the PO details match.
      • In the complete video of the machine being taken out of the packaging, the SN code on the machine body can be seen, and the SN code does not match the SN code on the shipping outer packaging/the SN code on the package tracking system.
  • When the user returns the item in the original packaging, and all the shipping information is retained (the shipping label, SN code, etc):
    • If the following four points can be proven, annotate as follows: `[All items have not been received (or none of the returned items are the purchased items)]`, otherwise annotate as `[Received all/part of the purchased items]`. (If they provide the original package as their supporting documents, it can be used as a reference also.)
      • The complete video of the machine being taken out of the packaging (the video should include the shipping label, return label, outer packaging SN code, and machine SN code).
      • The shipping package tracking number and the PO details match.
      • The return package tracking number and the PO details match.
      • The SN code on the machine does not match the SN code on the shipping outer packaging.

15. Annotation Rules for Furniture

Purpose: To assess the return of furniture items and determine the refund rate based on condition and specific issues.

Annotation & Evidence:

Damage Level Description & Criteria Evidence Required Maximum Deduction
1. Intact Product The product is intact, or the missing parts are low-cost and easy to replace (such as a few screws, nuts, standard connectors, or a user manual). No additional evidence is required (though the merchant may verify the original list of included parts). 0%
2. Appearance-Only Damage Only appearance affected: Functionality, structural stability, or safety not impacted. Examples: Minor surface scratches or light scuff marks, minor wear or fading of the paint surface (small areas), minor pilling or thread pulls in the fabric (not widespread), slight and recoverable deformation (e.g., minor indentations in cushions), small stains (can be cleaned). Photo or video showing the product with its original packaging, photo of the return shipping label, clear close-up photo or video of the damaged area (damage details must be visible). 10%
3. Minor Damage The item has limited localized damage (12 spots), typically repairable at low cost. Examples: Localized breakage of the support structure (e.g., a crack in a chair leg or a broken table corner), noticeable surface damage, dents, or cracks, localized collapse of filling materials (e.g., one area of a sofa cushion), severe localized contamination (cannot be cleaned). Photo or video showing the product with its original packaging, photo of the return shipping label, clear close-up photo or video of the damaged area, showing the exact location, quantity, and extent of the damage. 20%
4. Moderate Damage Multiple damaged areas (3 or more) that affect functionality, structural stability, or safety. Examples: Multiple surface damages, cracks, or severe dents, multiple breakages or deformations in the support structure, affecting stability, large-area collapse or exposure of filling materials, severe contamination covering a large area. Photo or video of the product with its original packaging, photo of the return shipping label, clear close-up photo or video of the main damaged areas, showing multiple points of damage. 35%
5. Severe Damage The item is beyond repair or resale, with serious impact on functionality, stability, or safety. Examples: Severe damage, breakage, or disassembly of the main structure, severely damaged surface beyond repair, significant loss or severe contamination of filling materials. Photo or video of the product with its original packaging, photo of the return package's shipping label, clear close-up photo or video of the severely damaged area, showing the critical damage that caused the item to be unsalvageable. 50%
6. Missing or Damaged Key Components Missing or unusable critical connectors or parts that affect assembly, core functions, safety, or normal usesuch as support legs, armrests, back panels, door panels, drawer fronts, motors (for electric furniture), remote controls, or specialized metal hardware. Photo or video of the product with its original packaging, photo of the return shipping label, close-up photo or video of the damaged component, or a reference image showing its original condition (to confirm the missing or damaged part).
  • 1 main component missing/damaged: 15% deduction
  • 2 main components missing/damaged: 30% deduction
  • 3 or more main components missing/damaged: up to 30% deduction

16. Wig Seller Rules

Key Steps:

  • Step 1: Check Mall ID and Seller Status:

Advanced Review Procedures

Image Proof for Chargeback Reversal Customer Service Review

Key Steps:

  • Step 1: Determine whether the image is a bank-issued document.
    • If the image is verified as a document issued by a bank or financial institution: Select "It is a bank document".
    • If not: Select "It is not a bank document".
    • Check Point: The image clearly shows that it is issued by a bank or financial institution.
  • Step 2: Does the image indicate that the chargeback has been reversed or failed?
    • If the image clearly shows the outcome (e.g., chargeback has been reversed, closed, or failed): Select "Chargeback reversal or failure shown".
    • If the image does not show the outcome: Select "Chargeback reversal or failure not shown".
    • Common Phrases:
      • Chargeback Reversed: Look for phrases such as "Chargeback Withdrawn", "Chargeback Cancelled", or "Dispute Cancelled".
      • Chargeback Failed: Look for phrases such as "Chargeback Representment Successful", "Dispute Ruled in Merchant's Favor", or "Dispute Lost by Cardholder".
  • Step 3: Does the image show signs of forgery (such as editing marks or images taken from the internet)?
    • If there are no obvious signs of forgery: Select "Signs of forgery not found".
    • If the uploaded image is clearly a non-genuine photo from the internet or shows obvious Photoshop marks: Select "Signs of forgery found".
  • Step 4: Is the chargeback amount in the image less than or equal to the payment amount?
    • Check Point: Customer service should compare the chargeback amount in the image with the user's actual payment amount.
    • If the chargeback amount is less than or equal to the payment amount: Select "Chargeback amount less than or equal to payment amount".
    • If it is greater than the payment amount, or the payment amount is not shown in the image: Select "Chargeback amount greater than payment amount".
  • Step 5: Is the chargeback time in the image later than the payment time?
    • Check Point: Customer service should compare the chargeback date in the image with the user's actual payment date.
    • If the chargeback date is later than or equal to the payment date: Select "Chargeback later than or concurring with payment".
    • If it is earlier: Select "Chargeback earlier than payment".

Return and refund by self-return - Logistics exception

The merchant has declined the refund request and submitted proof.

Key Steps:

  • Step 1: Compare the tracking number provided by the user with the one submitted by the merchant:
    • a. If the tracking numbers match: Proceed to Step 2.
    • b. If the tracking numbers do not match: Reject the merchant.
    • Check Point: Check whether the tracking number provided by the merchant on the logistics website matches the return tracking number shown in the Workbench.
  • Step 2: Check whether the tracking number provided by the user has been used previously:
    • a. If the tracking number has been used: Support the merchant.
    • b. If the tracking number has not been used: Proceed to Step 3.
    • Proof Requirements:
      • The merchant must provide a full screenshot of the official logistics tracking page, with all text clearly visible. The tracking number must be included in the image.
      • The merchant must also provide a full screenshot of the after-sales request details page showing the same tracking number. The content must be clear and legible.
    • Check Point: You should then check whether the same tracking number is used for different orders.
  • Step 3: Check in the tracking information if the package is marked as delivered:
    • a. If delivered: Proceed to Step 6.
    • b. If not delivered: Proceed to Step 4.
    • Check Point: Check the tracking status to see if the package has been delivered.
  • Step 4: Check in the tracking information if the package has not been picked up or shows no further tracking after pickup:
    • a. If not picked up or no further tracking after pickup: Support the merchant.
    • b. If picked up and there is further tracking: Proceed to Step 5.
    • Proof Requirements: The merchant must provide a complete screenshot of the logistics tracking from the official website. The text in the image must be clear and visible, showing no further tracking information other than the creation or pickup of the shipment.
  • Step 5: Check whether the latest tracking status shows any issues (e.g., package lost, shipping suspended, or no updates for over 7 calendar days):
    • a. If there are tracking issues: Support the merchant.
    • b. If tracking is normal and updating: Reject the merchant.
    • Proof Requirements: The merchant must provide a complete screenshot of the official tracking information, with clearly visible and identifiable tracking details. Either of the following conditions must be met:
      • The most recent tracking update is more than 7 calendar days old, with no further updates.
      • An official notice or statement from the carrier regarding tracking disruption, or a clear screenshot of a confirmed shipping suspension from the official website.
  • Step 6: Compare the delivery address with the warehouse address used by the user at the time of return:
    • a. If the addresses match or the distance is less than 5 miles (8 km): Reject the merchant.
    • b. If the distance is greater than 5 miles (8 km): Support the merchant.
    • Proof Requirements:
      • The merchant must provide a complete screenshot of the official tracking information. The image must include the final delivery address, which must be clearly visible and show the full detailed address. The tracking number must be included in the image.
      • The merchant must provide a screenshot of the after-sales details page clearly showing the warehouse information. The product details in the screenshot must match those in the user's request.
      • The merchant must provide a screenshot showing the distance between the delivery address and the warehouse address. The image must clearly show a distance of over 5 miles (8 km). (You may also verify this yourself.) Make sure the addresses match the warehouse address and the return package's delivery address.
    • Important Notes on Address Matching:
      • If the country, province, and city of the delivery address match the warehouse address, the proof must show the exact building and include the corresponding tracking number. Otherwise, the addresses are considered consistent, and the merchant will be rejected.
      • If the country, province, or city differs, you may select "Delivery and warehouse addresses are at least 5 miles (8 km) apart" to support the merchant.
